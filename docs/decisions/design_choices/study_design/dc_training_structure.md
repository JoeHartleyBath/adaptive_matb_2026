# Design choice: practice structure prior to calibration blocks

**Decision**  
Adopt an initial practice structure consisting of three short practice blocks (~5 minutes each) spanning increasing workload levels (`LOW` → `MODERATE` → `HIGH`), separated by brief quiet breaks, prior to calibration data collection.

**Rationale**  
- A structured practice phase prior to calibration data collection is common in MATB(-II) studies and is recommended in recent reviews to reduce learning effects and ensure task familiarization.
- The literature reports substantial variability in practice duration and structure, with no clear consensus on an optimal practice length, suggesting that practice parameters should be treated as design choices rather than fixed standards.
- Several MATB(-II) studies use short repeated practice trials (≈5 minutes) and expose participants to different workload levels during practice, supporting the use of brief, structured blocks as a practical familiarization strategy.
- An ascending workload order during practice provides a predictable ramp-up that may reduce early overload risk while exposing participants to the full task ecology.

**Alternatives considered**  
- No formal practice phase prior to calibration blocks.
- Different practice durations or block counts (e.g., fewer longer blocks or more shorter blocks).
- Different ordering strategies (e.g., randomized or descending workload).

**Implications**  
- practice exists as a distinct, non-calibration phase prior to experimental blocks and must be explicitly logged and labelled.
- Exact practice parameters (block count, duration, ordering) are considered pilot-tuned and may be revised following pilot evaluation without invalidating downstream analyses or contracts.

**Status**  
Proposed (pilot-tuned)
