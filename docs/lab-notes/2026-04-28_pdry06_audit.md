## Key anomalies identified

Two distinct anomalies were observed:

1. **Live adaptation saturation**: live MWL estimator output locked at p_high ≈ 0.97–1.0 throughout
   the adaptation run (`assist_on` fired at t = 3.9 s and never turned off; 474/474 audit ticks
   above threshold).
2. **Control offline saturation**: fresh-filter offline re-inference on the control XDF gives
   p_high ≈ 0.977 (97.7% above threshold). The model classifies control EEG as HIGH for almost the
   entire run.

Both conditions also show an anomalously low LORO J (0.249), indicating weak calibration
discrimination.

### Step 9 — Raw EEG amplitude check

Script: `scripts/_tmp_amplitude_check_pdry06_quick.py`

To confirm the feature deviations were not caused by an equipment failure (electrode pop-off,
impedance collapse, gain change), global RMS was computed from the raw (pre-filter, pre-decimate)
EEG in each condition:

| Condition | global RMS | ratio vs cal |
|---|---|---|
| rest | 0.01496 | 0.970× |
| cal_c1 | 0.01546 | 1.002× |
| cal_c2 | 0.01539 | 0.998× |
| control | 0.01506 | 0.976× |
| adaptation | 0.01486 | 0.963× |

**All conditions are within ±4% of the calibration mean.** The raw EEG amplitude is stable
throughout the session. Equipment failure, impedance collapse, and gain shift are ruled out.

The 20–40σ feature deviations found in Step 7 therefore arise from the **spectral/complexity
content** of the signal rather than from its amplitude. The norm_stats baseline (derived from
resting EEG) captures a very different spectral state: at rest, beta power and Hjorth Activity
are substantially lower than during any active task. The control MATB run suppressed beta
(very low beta z-score), while the adaptation run elevated it. Both conditions fall far from
the rest-baseline norm_stats reference point, and the SVM extrapolates well beyond its training
distribution in both directions.

### Step 10 — 25 Hz spectral artifact

Scripts: `scripts/_tmp_spectral_plot_pdry06.py` (raw), `scripts/_tmp_spectral_plot_pdry06.py` (filtered)

Spectral plots of all conditions were generated using Welch PSD, first on raw EEG then on
pipeline-filtered (0.5–40 Hz bandpass, 50 Hz notch) EEG. A narrow spike at approximately 25 Hz
is present in all conditions **after filtering** — it survives the existing preprocessing and
falls directly within the beta band (13–30 Hz) used by FM_Beta, Cen_Beta, and Hjorth Activity.

Critically, the spike amplitude varies markedly across recording conditions:

| Condition | 25 Hz spike relative to calibration |
|---|---|
| Calibration C1 | reference |
| Calibration C2 | reference |
| Control | ~10× **lower** than calibration |
| Adaptation | ~10× **higher** than calibration |

This variation maps directly onto the FM_Beta feature time series (control ≈ −40–50σ below rest,
adaptation ≈ +15–20σ above rest). The 25 Hz artifact is almost certainly a **non-neural electrical
source** in the recording environment (UK mains half-harmonic from a 50 Hz supply: monitors,
laptop chargers, fluorescent lights on dimmers, switching power supplies). Its amplitude is not
stable across sessions or conditions.

A notch at 25 Hz cannot be added to the production pipeline (it bisects the beta band). The
correct remediation is to **identify and eliminate the source** in the lab before participant runs.

Figures: `results/figures/spectral_pdry06.png` (raw),
`results/figures/spectral_filtered_pdry06.png` (post-filter, spike visible)

### Step 11 — 25 Hz artifact removal simulation

Script: `scripts/_tmp_simulate_25hz_fix_pdry06.py`

Three variants were compared for control and adaptation inference:

| Variant | Threshold method | Threshold | Control % HIGH | Adaptation % HIGH | Control mean p | Adap mean p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — deployed | LORO (live) | 0.4849 | **97.7%** | 14.2% | 0.959 | 0.243 |
| B — threshold only | 10-fold CV, no notch | 0.3285 | 99.0% | 29.5% | 0.959 | 0.243 |
| C — notch + re-cal | 10-fold CV, 25 Hz notched | 0.3363 | 79.8% | **96.6%** | 0.742 | 0.846 |

**Interpretation:**

- Variants A and B: changing only the threshold has no meaningful effect. Control remains
  saturated at >97% HIGH; adaptation remains far lower. The model's *direction* of classification
  is inverted — it reads control as higher MWL than adaptation in the full session.
- Variant C (IIR notch at 25 Hz Q=50 applied after EegPreprocessor, full recalibration including
  new norm_stats from notched rest XDF): **the inversion resolves**. Adaptation is now correctly
  identified as higher MWL (96.6% > threshold) than control (79.8%). Mean p_high ordering flips
  to the expected direction (adaptation 0.85 > control 0.74).
- FM_Beta z-scores (mid-session single window):
  - Control: deployed z = −45.2  →  notched z = −22.1
  - Adaptation: deployed z = +17.8  →  notched z = +11.1
  - Still large, but the notched model was calibrated on notched data and correctly handles this range.
- FM_Beta norm_std doubles with the notch (0.0465 → 0.0973), reflecting the rest XDF now showing
  a more realistic within-session variance without the spike.
- Notched 10-fold CV calibration was clean: all 10 folds J > 0.52, mean J = 0.620 (threshold = 0.3363).

**Conclusion: the 25 Hz electrical artifact is the confirmed root cause of the saturation
inversion for PDRY06.** The fix is physical, not algorithmic.

Figure: `results/figures/sim_25hz_fix_pdry06.png`

## Open question: live vs offline divergence (unexplained)

Even accepting that the EEG is out of distribution, the live estimator produced p_high ≈ 0.97
while offline re-inference on the same XDF EEG gives p_high ≈ 0.24. These two readings are the
same features, same model — yet diverge by ~0.73 in probability.

No hypothesis tested so far explains this gap. Remaining candidates that could not be tested
without real-time logs:

1. **LSL vs XDF amplitude mismatch** — if the live stream data arriving at the estimator had a
   different amplitude than what LabRecorder recorded, the feature vectors would differ. Could
   result from gain settings, clipping, or interleaving artefacts in dual-amp merge. Requires
   `--debug-verbose` logging or a direct byte-level comparison of LSL samples vs XDF samples.
2. **Estimator clock / timestamp misalignment** — if the estimator's ring buffer was pulling a
   different window than what the XDF time stamps map to, it would be inferring on a different
   slice of data. Unlikely given the persistent-filter design, but unverified.

To resolve this, the next dry run with MWL adaptation should launch the estimator with
`--debug-verbose` so per-window feature vectors are logged to file.

## Conclusions

1. **PDRY06 should not be used to validate the adaptation mechanism.** The EEG signal in the
   experimental conditions is contaminated by a 25 Hz electrical artifact whose amplitude varies
   10× between control and adaptation. This alone is sufficient to explain the FM_Beta deviation
   (−40–50σ control, +15–20σ adaptation). The artifact source must be identified and eliminated
   before real participant runs.
2. **The model architecture is not the problem.** Filter contamination, norm_stats errors, and
   audit logging bugs are all ruled out. The pipeline is working correctly; the input EEG is not.
3. **calibrate_participant.py has been updated** to use 10-fold stratified CV for threshold
   selection (replacing LORO pooled argmax). LORO J ≥ 0.10 is retained as a quality gate only.
   For PDRY06 the new threshold would have been 0.3285 (J=0.630) vs 0.4849 (J=0.249) deployed.
4. **Before the next participant, identify and eliminate the 25 Hz artifact source.** Candidate
   sources: monitor refresh rate, laptop charger, fluorescent lighting on a dimmer. Check with
   the amplifier on battery and all non-essential equipment powered off.
5. **The online/offline p_high divergence remains unexplained.** Enable `--debug-verbose` on the
   next adaptation run to capture per-window feature vectors from the live estimator.
